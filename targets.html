Our Targets

Health for everyone - no exceptions!

Target 3.1

It aims to significantly reduce the global maternal mortality ratio to fewer than 70 deaths per 100,000 live births by 2030. This target underscores the need for universal access to high-quality maternal healthcare, including skilled attendance during pregnancy, childbirth, and the postnatal period.Its overarching objective is to ensure that every woman can experience pregnancy and childbirth safely, supported by effective, timely, and equitable healthcare services.

Target 3.4

It aims to reduce premature mortality from non-communicable diseases (NCDs)—such as cardiovascular diseases, cancer, diabetes, depression, and chronic respiratory illnesses—by one third by 2030, through prevention and treatment. It also emphasizes the promotion of mental health and well-being as essential components of overall health.

Target 3.b

It focuses on supporting research and development for vaccines and medicines that address both communicable and non-communicable diseases, particularly those affecting low- and middle-income countries. It also aims to ensure affordable and equitable access to essential medicines and vaccines for all.
